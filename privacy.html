Privacy Policy

Effective: June 22, 2026 · Last updated: June 22, 2026

Demo notice. ShopWave is a portfolio demo. The policy below is written as a real furniture studio's would be, but no real orders, payments, or customer data are processed on this site.

At ShopWave, we believe a furniture studio earns your trust the same way it earns the right to live in your home — slowly, honestly, and over time. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you visit shopwave.com or buy from us, how we use it, who we share it with, and the choices you have. By using our site or services, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Who we are

ShopWave is a furniture design and manufacturing studio. In this policy, "ShopWave," "we," "our," and "us" refer to ShopWave Studio. "You" means anyone who visits our website, contacts us, or buys a piece from our collection. The data controller responsible for your information is ShopWave Studio.

2. Information we collect

Information you provide to us

When you create an account, place an order, sign up for our newsletter, request a swatch kit, or contact our customer care team, we collect the information you give us. This typically includes your name, email address, shipping and billing addresses, phone number, payment details (processed by our payment partners — we never store full card numbers on our servers), and any messages or photos you send us.

Information we collect automatically

When you browse our site, we automatically collect technical information such as your device type, browser, IP address, operating system, referring URL, pages viewed, links clicked, and approximate location (country and region). This helps us improve the site, troubleshoot issues, and protect against fraud.

Information from third parties

We may receive information from our shipping carriers (delivery status), payment processors (transaction confirmation), social platforms (if you interact with us there), and trusted analytics partners. We only use what we need to fulfill your order and serve you well.

3. How we use your information

  • To process and deliver your orders, including white-glove delivery scheduling.
  • To honor our lifetime craftsmanship guarantee and provide repair or replacement service.
  • To respond to questions, design consultations, and customer-care requests.
  • To send order confirmations, shipping updates, and (with your consent) marketing emails about new collections.
  • To personalize what you see on our site and recommend pieces you may love.
  • To detect, prevent, and respond to fraud, abuse, or security incidents.
  • To comply with our legal and tax obligations.

4. How we share your information

We do not sell your personal information. We share it only with carefully chosen partners who need it to deliver our service:

  • Service providers — payment processors, fulfillment warehouses, shipping carriers, white-glove delivery teams, email and SMS platforms, and customer-care tools.
  • Professional advisors — accountants, auditors, and legal counsel, under strict confidentiality.
  • Authorities — when required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of ShopWave, our customers, or the public.
  • Business transfers — if ShopWave is ever sold, merged, or restructured, your information may transfer to the new entity, subject to this policy.

5. Cookies and tracking technologies

We use cookies, pixels, and similar technologies to keep you signed in, remember items in your cart, measure how our site is used, and improve your experience. You can manage cookies through your browser settings or our cookie preferences center. Disabling some cookies may affect site functionality such as your cart or saved addresses.

6. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you have the right to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict the use of your personal information, and to withdraw consent at any time. You can also unsubscribe from marketing emails using the link at the bottom of any message.

For California residents (CCPA / CPRA)

You have the right to know what personal information we collect, request its deletion, correct inaccurate information, and limit the use of sensitive personal information. We do not sell your personal information.

For residents of the EEA and UK (GDPR)

You have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, and port your personal data, and to object to processing. You may also lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@shopwave-demo.example. We will respond within the timeframe required by law (typically 30 days).

7. Data retention

We keep your information only as long as we need it for the purposes described in this policy — generally for the life of your account, plus seven years for tax and warranty records. After that, we securely delete or anonymize it.

8. Data security

Our site is served over HTTPS and protected by industry-standard safeguards. We work with PCI-compliant payment processors and limit access to your personal information to staff who need it to do their jobs. No system is ever 100% secure, but we work hard to keep your data safe.

9. International data transfers

If you visit us from outside the country where our servers are located, your information may be transferred to and processed in another country. We rely on standard contractual clauses and equivalent safeguards to protect your information when it travels.

10. Children's privacy

Our site is intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has provided us their information, please contact us and we will delete it.

11. Third-party links

Our site may link to third-party websites — for example, our Instagram, designer collaborators, or shipping carriers. This policy does not apply to those sites. Please review their privacy practices before sharing your information.

12. Changes to this policy

As our studio grows, this policy may change. We will post any updates here with a new "Last updated" date. For significant changes, we will notify you by email or a prominent notice on our homepage.
